The opportunity

A market with almost no supply

Africa hosts ~0.6% of global data-centre capacity for ~19% of the world's population. Nigeria — Africa's largest economy — hosts ~90% of its data abroad and leaks roughly $1bn a year in cloud spend offshore. From 1 January 2027 the Central Bank requires all payment-transaction data stored in-country. Local AI compute is scarce, and the first at-scale GPU clusters are only now arriving.

*Payback assumes take-or-pay contracts covering at least the first ~3 years; the tail beyond contract tenor assumes re-contracting at then-current rates and is flagged when applicable. Uncontracted (merchant) revenue is exposed to rate decline: H200 rents are widely expected to fall 15–30%/yr as newer chips ramp (the H100 fell ~65% in two years; L40S-class inference rates are also drifting down ~10–15%/yr). "After refresh reserve" is EBITDA minus a straight-line reserve to replace GPUs every 5 years at 85% of cost (15% resale). Figures are unlevered and pre-ramp: Nigerian USD project debt runs ~10–12% (DFI/blended finance is the intended route — see assumptions), and first revenue follows an 18–30 month build plus a 6–12 month fill ramp. The default case is deliberately conservative — the levers that close the gap to a financeable yield are anchor take-or-pay at 75%+ utilisation, concessional DFI debt, and the managed-services layer running on the same capital.

Competitive position

The window is open — and measured in quarters

Africa's first at-scale AI cluster (Cassava × NVIDIA, 3,000 GPUs) went live in Cape Town in March 2026; its Nigeria deployment is planned but not yet live. In Lagos, Udutech's GPU Hub rents small-scale inference GPUs (live since Aug 2025, under $1/hr), Airtel Nxtra began going live around Q1 2026 (telco cloud and inference scale), MTN adds capacity H2 2026, and Kasi Cloud's first 5.5 MW commissions mid-2026. As of early July 2026, no publicly confirmed 1,000-plus-GPU training-class cluster operates in Nigeria — that is the gap this project fills. Moving now means being at scale when the CBN's January 2027 deadline forces every bank, fintech and switch to store and manage payment data in-country — making locally hosted AI on that data far simpler than shipping it offshore.

Worked example — one tier-2 bank. A fraud-scoring subscription at ~$120k/yr — a global-vendor-class product (the Actimize/Feedzai tier runs $150k–500k) at a 20–75% discount. Direct cost — inference compute, support and model upkeep — runs ~$20k/yr, leaving ~$100k contribution (~80%). So roughly 6–7 such clients cover the core engineering team, and each client after that lands at ~80% incremental margin. The realistic universe is ~26 commercial banks, 4 large telcos and 10–15 scaled fintechs — the model below caps clients accordingly. One prerequisite stated plainly: identity products require NIBSS/NIMC-licensed data access, which is the incumbents' real moat. Our fraud scoring, document AI and biometric liveness checks do not depend on it; verification against BVN/NIN reference photos is offered only via licensed partners, like lookups.
