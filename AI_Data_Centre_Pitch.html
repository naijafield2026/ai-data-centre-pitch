Three ways in

The same GPUs, three deployment paths

Each trades upfront capital against running cost and control. Start lean and scale, or go big from day one — the calculator below models all three.

Rent racks

Colocation — buy GPUs, rent space
Lowest capex · fastest launch
  • Live in weeks, minimal build
  • You own the GPUs (portable later)
  • Pay rack rent forever — best for a pilot

Build on the 15 MW grid

Existing power, no plant to build
Mid capex · no land/power plant
  • ~65% of capital goes to GPUs
  • Grid caps the site at ~8,900 GPUs
  • Pay a grid tariff instead of building generation

Build own facility

Self-powered (flared gas + solar)
Highest capex · cheapest power
  • Full control, no grid ceiling
  • Near-free flared-gas fuel = low opex
  • Best long-term economics at scale

Worked example — one mid-tier bank. A fraud-scoring subscription at ~$120k/yr. Compute to score its transactions on our AMD platform costs a few hundred dollars a month; support and model upkeep a few thousand. Contribution after direct cost is ~$100k/yr — so roughly 7 such clients cover the entire engineering team, and every client after that is nearly pure profit. This is why the model breaks even fast and then scales at 85%+ incremental margin.
